# 🔬 Research Plan — ABO Sim Evolution (v2.0)

## 1. Objective
Investigate the current state of the art in hematology educational software, pedagogical trends in medical simulation, and technical optimizations for web-based biological simulations to evolve **ABO Sim** from a "Typing Simulator" to a "Comprehensive Imunohematology Learning Platform".

## 2. Research Domains & Queries

### Domain A: Competitive Analysis (Educational Software)
*Goal: Identify existing tools and feature gaps.*
- **Queries**:
    - `"blood type simulator educational software"`
    - `"virtual blood typing lab"`
    - `"hematology simulation for high school students"`
    - `"interactive blood group learning tool"`
    - `"simulador de tipagem sanguínea online"`

### Domain B: Pedagogical Methodology (EdTech & MedEd)
*Goal: Apply modern learning theories (CBL, PBL, Gamification).*
- **Queries**:
    - `"Case-Based Learning (CBL) in medical simulation software"`
    - `"gamification strategies for biology education"`
    - `"active learning tools for hematology"`
    - `"scaffolding in medical educational apps"`
    - `"effective feedback loops in biological simulations"`

### Domain C: Scientific Depth (Advanced Imunohematology)
*Goal: Define the implementation of rare phenotypes and complex systems.*
- **Queries**:
    - `"teaching Bombay phenotype to high school students"`
    - `"A1 and A2 subgroups blood typing simulation"`
    - `"Kell and Duffy blood group systems simplified explanation"`
    - `"common misconceptions in blood group inheritance"`

### Domain D: Technical Optimization (JS/Canvas/HCI)
*Goal: Improve performance and accessibility.*
- **Queries**:
    - `"high performance particle system canvas 2d javascript"`
    - `"accessible medical simulations WCAG 2.2"`
    - `"adaptive learning algorithms for educational quizzes"`
    - `"lightweight physics engines for biological simulations"`

### Domain E: Open Source & Community (GitHub/Forums)
*Goal: Find reference implementations and community needs.*
- **Queries (GitHub)**:
    - `topic:hematology`
    - `blood-type simulator`
    - `medical-simulation javascript`
    - `blood-group-logic`

## 3. Evidence Graph Structure
The findings will be mapped into the following relations:
- `[Feature]` $\rightarrow$ `[Pedagogical Benefit]` $\rightarrow$ `[Source]`
- `[Technical Gap]` $\rightarrow$ `[Proposed Solution]` $\rightarrow$ `[Reference Implementation]`
- `[Scientific Concept]` $\rightarrow$ `[Simplified Model]` $\rightarrow$ `[Validation Source]`

## 4. Success Criteria
- Identification of at least 3 direct competitors or similar tools.
- Definition of a "Patient Journey" workflow based on CBL/PBL literature.
- Technical specification for the "Lite" mode and "Adaptive Quiz".
- Scientific blueprint for the Bombay phenotype simulation.

## 5. Timeline
- **Phase 2 (Research)**: 2-3 iterative cycles of searching and verification.
- **Phase 3 (Arbitration)**: Conflict resolution between sources.
- **Phase 4 (Analysis)**: Feature matrix comparison.
- **Phase 5 (Synthesis)**: Final Improvement Report.
